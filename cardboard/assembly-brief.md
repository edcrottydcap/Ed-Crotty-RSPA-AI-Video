# Cardboard Assembly Brief

> **What you're reading:** [Cardboard](https://www.usecardboard.com/) is an AI-powered video editor. Instead of manually cutting and splicing clips on a timeline, you upload raw footage and describe what you want in plain English. The AI handles the editing — trimming dead air, adding captions, adjusting pacing, and assembling clips into a polished final product.
>
> Below are the exact prompts Ed will use to turn raw demo recordings into the finished video. Tips sourced directly from the Cardboard team.

---

## Main Video (3-5 min, 16:9 landscape)

> I have multiple raw clips that need to be assembled into a single 3-5 minute video:
>
> 1. A talking head intro clip (~1-2 min raw) — this opens the video
> 2. Screen recording: Demo 1 — using Gemini to identify something from a photo
> 3. Screen recording: Demo 2 — uploading multiple files to Claude and generating a report
> 4. Screen recording: Demo 3 — uploading a hand-drawn sketch to ChatGPT and generating a professional image
> 5. Screen recording: Demo 4 — taking that generated image and creating a video with Gemini Veo
> 6. Phone video: Demo 5 — using Gemini Live with a phone camera to debug a payment pinpad in real-time
> 7. A talking head outro clip (~30 sec raw) — this closes the video
>
> Assemble in this exact order. Remove silences, add captions, and tighten pacing to 1.5x. Keep transitions clean — nothing flashy. Format: 16:9 landscape.

## Talking Head as Picture-in-Picture

> Add my talking-head footage as a picture-in-picture overlay during the screen recording demos. Keep captions on.

*Note: Use this if you want your face visible during demos rather than just voiceover. Cardboard handles the PiP compositing natively.*

## Individual Clips (for social media)

> From the same raw footage, create 5 individual short clips — one per demo, 30-60 seconds each. Each clip should stand alone with enough context that a viewer understands what they're seeing without the full video. Add captions to all clips.

For a more polished individual clip, try a structured prompt like:

> Create a 60-second clip from Demo [X]. Start with the AI interaction showing the product in use, then transition to a talking-head section. End with a 10-second reel-style closer.

## Vertical Cuts (optional)

> Also create 9:16 vertical versions of the 5 individual demo clips for Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts / TikTok.

## Cardboard Pro Tips (from the Cardboard team)

### Stacking Requests
You can chain multiple editing instructions in a single prompt. For example:
> "Remove silences, add captions, and tighten pacing to 1.5x."

Cardboard handles captions, pacing, transitions, and music — so focus your prompts on creative direction rather than technical details.

### Search by Content
Use Cardboard's search-by-content feature to find specific moments in your raw footage. Instead of scrubbing through timecodes, search for what happened:
> "The prompt where I generated the storefront scene"
> "The moment the pinpad error appears"
> "Where I uploaded the files to Claude"

This is especially useful when you have 30-45 min of raw footage and need to find a specific demo moment.

## Style Notes

- Tone: conversational, credible, practical — not hype
- Captions: always on (most social media views are muted)
- Pacing: tight, no dead air — use 1.5x tightening for snappy feel
- Transitions: clean and simple, nothing flashy
- Music: optional, subtle background if it fits — let Cardboard suggest
